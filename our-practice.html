    Meet Diana


    PMHNP-BC

    One hand I extend into myself, the other towards others

    A nurse who is scared of blood’, that is how my family always describes me, and I always tell them ‘That’s why I chose mental health’ but in reality, mental health chose me. My passion for mental health began when I started working with individuals with developmental disabilities in group homes as a direct care staff over 20 years ago. I quickly figured I could serve them better, thus my journey through nursing school. I became a delegating nurse in 2009, and eventually a PMHNP, to give people a better quality of care and access to care. I hold an Associate degree in Nursing from Howard Community College, a BSc. in Nursing from Stevenson University (Maryland), and a Masters in Nursing from Walden University (Minnesota). I’m well-traveled and, as a result, have reputable experience within this industry. I believe in transparency, effective communication, and treating people with respect. My favorite time is spent reading suspense novels and watching dramas with sub titles.
